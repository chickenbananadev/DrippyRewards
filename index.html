Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is $DRIPPY?
$DRIPPY is a Solana token that pays you to hold it. Every buy and sell carries a 5% tax. That tax is converted to SOL and distributed straight to holder wallets every 30 minutes. No staking, no claiming, no farming. Hold the dog, get paid.
How do the rewards actually work?
The 5% tax on every trade collects in the contract. Every 30 minutes the contract converts it to SOL and sends it to every eligible holder, weighted by how much $DRIPPY you hold. The SOL lands directly in your wallet. You do not need to do anything to receive it.
What makes the rewards bigger or smaller?
Rewards come entirely from the 5% tax on trades, so they scale with trading volume. Busy, high-volume periods fill the pool faster and mean bigger, more frequent drips. During quiet stretches with little trading, drips slow down until volume returns. It is real yield tied to real activity — the more the pack trades, the more everyone earns.
What is Burn-to-Earn?
When you burn your $DRIPPY tokens, you permanently remove them from supply. In return, your burned amount keeps earning rewards forever at a 2x weight. You give up the tokens but keep a doubled claim on every future distribution. For example, if you burn 100,000 $DRIPPY, every future SOL distribution pays your wallet as if it still held 200,000 — double the burned amount — for life, even after your balance hits zero. It is the highest conviction play in the ecosystem.
How do I burn my tokens?
Send $DRIPPY to the official burn address — N1LCBQJnjLP3ppv7npzL5Btzf5Yp3hBMr6s8GmVfEyV (also shown in the Burn section above) — or use the Burn Tokens function on the Forge token page. Either way the system records your burn and your wallet starts earning 2x on the burned amount. ⚠️ Only send $DRIPPY to this address; tokens sent here are burned forever and cannot be recovered.
How often do I get paid?
The distribution engine runs on a 30-minute cycle, around the clock — the timer at the top of this page counts down to the next one. Each cycle sends the SOL collected from that window's trading straight to holder wallets, automatically, weighted by your holdings. So the schedule is every 30 minutes; how big each drip is depends on trading volume (see "What makes the rewards bigger or smaller?" above).
Is there a minimum amount I need to hold?
Rewards are weighted by holdings, so larger bags earn larger cuts. Very small holders may receive tiny amounts that round down on a given cycle, but the system rewards everyone in the pack proportionally.
How do I buy $DRIPPY?
Use the swap widget on this page, or buy directly on ForgeSwap. You need a Solana wallet like Phantom or Solflare with some SOL in it. Connect, swap SOL for $DRIPPY, and you are in the pack.
Where can I see my earnings?
Use the wallet checker on this page. Paste your address and you will see your total SOL earned, payout history, current holdings, and burn weight. Every payout links to the transaction on Solscan so you can verify it on-chain.
Is the contract safe?
$DRIPPY launched on Forge, and all activity is verifiable on-chain. You can review the contract, distributions, and burns anytime on Solscan and the Forge token page. Always do your own research and never invest more than you can afford to lose.
Why does Bubblemaps show all the wallets as connected?
That is the rewards engine — not insiders. Because $DRIPPY automatically pays SOL to every holder wallet every 30 minutes, the distribution wallet has sent a transaction to nearly every holder. Clustering tools like Bubblemaps read those payout transactions as "connections" and draw links between all the wallets, which makes a normal, widely-held token look like one big cluster. It is an expected side effect of any auto-distributing rewards token, not a sign that one person controls the wallets. Every distribution is public and verifiable on Solscan, so you can trace exactly where the SOL came from.
